## Design Context

### Users
Conference and workshop attendees — primarily technical (security researchers, students, ML practitioners). They encounter the app at a booth or during a workshop session. They use it for 10–30 minutes, standing or sitting, on a laptop browser. State of mind: curious, slightly competitive, playful within a professional setting. They expect something that looks deliberate and credible, not a toy.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **controlled, precise, electric.**

The app presents itself like a serious professional tool — clean surfaces, crisp type, no decoration for decoration's sake. Then, when a user breaks the model, something electric happens. The contrast IS the design: 99% calm, 1% explosion. The name is "Jailbreaking Challenge" and the logo is typographic, set in a distinctive custom font — no imagery, no icons as decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Polished productivity tool with a single moment of drama.**

Think: Linear, Vercel dashboard, or a security console — quiet confidence, dense information without feeling cluttered, strong typographic hierarchy. Supports both light and dark mode; the user can toggle. 

- Light mode: near-white surface with warm-tinted neutrals, dark ink text, a single sharp accent color for interactive elements
- Dark mode: deep near-black surface (not pure black), slightly warm-tinted, the same accent pops more in darkness
- Accent: `#00665c` — a deep, dark teal. Used **sparingly**: interactive element borders on focus, the active level pill, the success glow on the chatbox. Not used for text, backgrounds, or decorative purposes. Most of the UI is neutral; the accent should feel like a purposeful signal, not a theme color.
- Typography: a geometric display font for the "Jailbreaking Challenge" wordmark; a refined, non-Inter sans-serif for UI; monospace only for the chatbot response output (earned, not decorative)
- No cards nested in cards, no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no rounded rectangles with generic shadows

### Layout & Composition
**The chatbox is the page.** There is one dominant element: a large, visually heavy rounded chatbox centered on the page (max-width ~700px on desktop). It is the axis around which everything else orbits. There is no sidebar, no panel grid, no card-in-card.

Above the chatbox, a few lines of instruction text clarify the goal immediately: no onboarding flow, no modal — just readable text on the page. The chatbox contains: a scrollable message thread, level pills (Easy / Medium / Hard) above the composer, and a pinned composer at the bottom.

The slim page header holds only: typographic wordmark left, theme toggle right, and a tiny GPU status dot. Nothing else — no nav, no stats link at the top. Stats are accessible via a low-prominence footer link only.

### Interaction Patterns
- Multi-turn chat with visible conversation history inside the chatbox
- Streaming: response text appears token by token as it arrives (SSE). Pulsing cursor during generation.
- Enter to send; Shift+Enter for newlines
- Level switch resets the conversation with a system message
- Success is auto-detected case-insensitively on the accumulated response

### Success Moment
**Remarkable but contained. Not disruptive.**  
When the secret word is revealed: the chatbox border and/or background briefly pulses with `#00665c` — a deep teal glow that fades over ~1.5s. A success message renders inline in the conversation thread, with the secret word visually highlighted in the accent color. No full-screen takeover, no confetti, no modal. The drama is in the chatbox itself, not over it. Users stay in context.

For failed attempts: a quiet "Not yet" dim badge appears after the AI response — same line, no color, no emphasis.

### Responsive / Mobile
**50% of users are on phones.** Mobile is a first-class target, not an afterthought.
- Chatbox fills near-full viewport width at screens < 768px
- Composer textarea stacks to full width; send button below or right
- Level pills wrap if needed
- Message bubbles reflowed for narrow viewports
- Minimum tap target: 44×44px for all interactive elements
- No horizontal scroll at 375px viewport width

### What Is Hidden
- Stats page: accessible only via a small "Stats" text link in the page footer. Not a nav item. Not mentioned in the main UI hierarchy.
- Background logging: full conversation history is saved server-side (anonymous), not exposed anywhere in the UI.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint everywhere except the moment of success.** Everything is calm and functional until the secret word is revealed — then the chatbox earns the right to glow, briefly.
2. **The chatbox is the product.** Every design decision services it. Nothing competes for attention.
3. **Typography does the heavy lifting.** Hierarchy, weight, and spacing carry the UI. No redundant icons above headings.
4. **The tool should feel inevitable.** Every affordance is where you expect it. No clever surprises in navigation or layout — only in the challenge content itself.
5. **Both themes are first-class.** Light and dark modes are designed together, not one retrofitted from the other.
6. **Conference-grade legibility.** Contrast ratios exceed WCAG AA. Must read clearly on a laptop screen under fluorescent lighting or in a dim workshop room. Must be usable one-handed on a phone.

### Bans (do not use)
- No full-screen overlays or modals on success
- No glassmorphism, no gradient text, no nested cards, no side-stripe borders
- No neon/bright cyan, no purple, no AI color palette. (The accent `#00665c` is a deliberate exception — dark, desaturated, used sparingly only.)
- No floating action buttons
- No bottom navigation bars
- No onboarding flows or tooltips
